1. Misconduct and Sportsmanship
Substance Use: Consuming alcohol or banned substances during a competitive round.

Vandalism & Safety Risks: Deliberately causing serious damage to the putting green, throwing clubs, or endangering other players and spectators.

Cheating: Deliberately ignoring a rule to gain an advantage, or mutually agreeing with a competitor to waive a rule.

Disruptive Behavior: Repeatedly using vulgar language, intentionally distracting competitors during a stroke, or altering the course setup (such as moving tee-markers or boundary stakes) without permission.

2. Scoring and Eligibility Violations
Handicap Manipulation: Using an inaccurate or manipulated handicap index to gain an unfair advantage in net-score events.

Incorrect Scorecard Submission: Returning a scorecard with a handicap that is too high, or signing for a hole score lower than what was actually taken.

Ineligible Entry: Entering an event without meeting the tournament's specific entry criteria or official club affiliation requirements.

3. Equipment and Transport Breaches
Non-Conforming Clubs: Making a stroke with a club that does not meet official equipment specifications (e.g., illegal driver heads or non-compliant groove layouts).

Non-Conforming Balls: Playing a ball that is not on the official list of approved and conforming golf balls.

Unauthorized Transport: Using a motorized golf cart during a round without prior written tournament authorization or a valid medical dispensation.

4. Timeliness and Weather Procedures
Lateness: Failing to start at the exact scheduled tee time. Arriving more than 5 minutes late results in automatic disqualification.

Ignoring Emergency Sirens: Failing to discontinue play immediately when a suspension siren sounds for imminent danger, such as lightning.

Refusing Official Tests: Refusing to submit to required administrative or wellness screenings mandated for the event.
